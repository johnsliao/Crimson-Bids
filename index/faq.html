Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)


What is Crimson Bids?

Crimson Bids is an online platform aimed to simplify your process to acquire inventory for your eBay business. This tool helps you find high quality auction/inventory products using language processing and by screening out illegitimate listings.

You can read more on the about page.


How does Crimson Bids work?

Crimson Bids takes many many eBay listings and pushes it through a set of requirements (e.g. seller must have a feedback rating greater than 95%). Then Crimson Bids uses language processing to analyze the seller description and seller notes to categorize specifics about each listing (e.g. if a phone has a cracked screen or if it comes with its original box).

Listings are then organized for your convenience as a individual buyer or eBay business.


Are these eBay listings up live?

Yes. All listings are updated every 10 minutes, so you can get the latest auction/inventory information.


What is the difference between "like-new" and "for-parts"?

Like-new: Products that have been lightly used but are in pristine or mint condition. Products that fall under this category fulfill a stringent set of requirements. For example, for an iPhone product, the physical condition of the phone will have no damage, all functions will be working, and the ESN/IMEID/ICLOUD will be unlocked.

For-parts: Products that can be refurbished or disassembled for individual sale/replacement parts. These products have damaged or cracked screens, the ESN/IMEID/ICLOUD may be locked, or some physical functions may not be working (e.g. camera broken).
